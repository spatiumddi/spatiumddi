## Executive summary

Of the 70 issues triaged, 5 are likely closable now — three are already shipped (#151 smart rollback, #159 nftables drop-in, #30 UniFi) and two are decline-or-wontdo (#163 node-exporter, #133 README sweep is build-ready but recommended decline). The bulk of the list — roughly 30 issues — are genuine build candidates marked should=recommend, and of those a strong tier of 18 quick wins are build-ready at S/M effort with the best ROI (reporting primitives, RBAC time-bounding, ops tooling, appliance host-config, and copilot/integration follow-ups). Eight big bets are recommend-but-expensive (L/XL): ACME embedded client (#28), in-app help (#97), copilot write tools (#98), address sets (#103), TLS cert monitoring (#118), the appliance k3s follow-ups (#193), control-plane HA (#272 — already partial), and the fleet firewall validation (#353). The single dominant pattern is a large appliance host-config shelf (#155–#166, #172–#174, #281, #283, #300, #149, #161) all riding the same ConfigBundle → trigger-file → host-runner plumbing — these should be designed as one program, not piece-by-piece. A second cluster is the read-only integration shelf (#30–#38, #68) where most tier-2 sources are deferred behind a shared reconciler shape. Reporting/analytics (#43–#48, #53) and UX polish (#77–#82, #97) each form coherent groups where a few high-value items (utilization history #44, decom dates #46, top-N #47, compliance PDF #48) are worth pulling forward and the rest deferring. Notably many "needs-design" verdicts cluster in RBAC/workflow (#62–#66, #103) and the immutable-rootfs appliance future (#139, #140, #142, #281) — these are architecturally heavy and should stay in design until a concrete deployment forces them.
